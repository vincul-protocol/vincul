# Vincul Protocol — Glossary
`spec/GLOSSARY.md` · Draft v0.1

---

## Purpose

Shared vocabulary precedes shared code.

This glossary defines the canonical terms used throughout the Vincul
specification. All implementations, documentation, and discussions
must use these terms consistently.

Where a term has a common usage that differs from its Vincul definition,
the Vincul definition takes precedence within this protocol.

Ambiguity in vocabulary is a protocol violation before it becomes a bug.

---

## Core Terms

---

### Agent

A software process that acts on behalf of a single human Principal
within boundaries that Principal has explicitly authorized.

An Agent may:
- Negotiate with other Agents via VinculNet
- Execute actions within its authorized Capability scope
- Generate Receipts for every action taken
- Participate in one or more Coalitions simultaneously

An Agent may not:
- Act beyond the Capability scope granted by its Principal
- Execute actions on behalf of a Coalition without a valid Coalition Contract
- Generate Receipts that misrepresent Intent, Authority, or Result

An Agent is not autonomous. It is bounded.
The distinction is the foundation of Vincul.

---

### Principal

The human whose intent an Agent represents.

A Principal:
- Holds a cryptographic identity within VinculNet
- Issues consent to their Agent explicitly and revocably
- Is the sole source of authority for their Agent's actions
- Receives all Receipts generated by their Agent

A Coalition has no Principal. It has Signatories.
This distinction matters: no single human owns a Coalition.

---

### Boundary

An explicit, machine-verifiable limit on authority.

A Boundary defines:
- **Who** may act (Agent identity)
- **What** they may act on (Capability scope)
- **Why** they may act (stated Purpose)
- **When** they may act (temporal scope)
- **How far** they may act (budget ceiling, action limit, or threshold)

A Boundary without all five dimensions is incomplete and invalid.

Boundaries are the structural unit of trust in Vincul.
Everything else is built on top of them.

---

### Coalition

A temporary, purpose-bound group of Principals who have composed
their consent into a shared Coalition Contract.

A Coalition:
- Has a defined Purpose
- Has a finite set of Signatories
- Operates within a Coalition Contract
- Dissolves automatically upon Purpose fulfillment or expiry
- May spawn Sub-coalitions within its own Boundary
- May join a Parent Coalition, inheriting its constraints

A Coalition is not a company.
It is not a legal entity.
It is a bounded, self-executing, ephemeral coordination structure.

---

### Coalition Contract

The machine-readable, human-legible document that defines a Coalition's
Boundary and governs all actions taken within it.

A Coalition Contract must specify:

| Field | Description |
|---|---|
| `contract_id` | Globally unique identifier |
| `purpose` | Plain-language statement of intent |
| `signatories` | List of Principal identifiers |
| `capabilities` | Scoped permissions granted to the Coalition |
| `budget` | Optional spending ceiling per Signatory and/or total |
| `vote_threshold` | Fraction required to authorize actions above threshold |
| `expiry` | Absolute timestamp of automatic dissolution |
| `dissolution_rules` | Conditions for early termination |
| `parent_contract` | Optional — if this is a Sub-coalition |

A Coalition Contract becomes valid when all Signatories have
affirmatively signed it. Partial signing confers no authority.

A Coalition Contract is immutable once valid.
Amendments require a new Contract with full re-signing.

---

### Signatory

A Principal who has affirmatively signed a Coalition Contract.

A Signatory:
- Consents to the Coalition's stated Purpose
- Grants the Coalition the right to act within its defined Capabilities
- Is independently verifiable as having consented
- May revoke consent within the terms defined by the Contract

Signatories are not equal by default.
Vote thresholds, budget allocations, and role assignments
may differentiate them within the Contract.

A Signatory is not responsible for actions taken by the Coalition
beyond the boundaries they consented to.
This is the legal and ethical core of bounded authority.

---

### Capability

A named, scoped permission that defines what an Agent or Coalition
may do within a specific domain.

Capabilities follow a hierarchical naming convention:

```
domain.resource:action
```

Examples:
```
calendar.freebusy:read
calendar.events:write
gmail.inbox:read
filesystem.documents:read
payment.budget:spend
```

Capabilities are:
- Explicit — never implied
- Compositional — a Coalition's Capabilities are the intersection
  of what each Signatory has granted, not the union
- Revocable — independently of other Capabilities

A Coalition cannot possess a Capability that any Signatory
has not individually granted.
Capability inheritance is always restrictive, never expansive.

---

### Receipt

The canonical, immutable record of a single collective action.

Every action taken under a Coalition Contract must produce a Receipt.
A Receipt is not a log entry. It is a structured claim about what happened,
under whose authority, and with what result.

**Structure:**

```
Receipt {
  receipt_id:       globally unique identifier
  contract_id:      the Coalition Contract that authorized this action
  timestamp:        ISO 8601 UTC

  intent: {
    description:    plain-language statement of what was attempted
    requested_by:   Principal or Agent that initiated
  }

  authority: {
    contract_id:    reference to governing Coalition Contract
    signatories:    list of Principals whose consent applies
    capability:     the specific Capability exercised
    vote_record:    if threshold required — who voted, when, result
    threshold_met:  boolean
  }

  result: {
    status:         succeeded | failed | partial | reverted
    description:    plain-language outcome
    artifacts:      list of produced artifacts (bookings, documents, etc.)
    budget_impact:  spend amount and remaining budget if applicable
    reversible:     boolean
    revert_by:      timestamp deadline for reversal, if reversible
  }
}
```

A Receipt must be:
- **Deterministic** — identical inputs produce identical Receipts
- **Symmetric** — every Signatory receives the same Receipt
- **Portable** — valid across all conforming implementations
- **Immutable** — once issued, a Receipt cannot be altered

A Receipt is not evidence of correctness.
It is evidence of authorship.

### VinculNet

The transport and identity protocol that enables Agents to discover,
authenticate, and communicate with each other.

VinculNet provides:
- **Agent identity** — asymmetric keypair per Agent, human-verifiable
- **Pairing** — explicit out-of-band verification before trust is established
- **Transport** — direct or relayed communication with NAT traversal
- **Discovery** — Agent endpoint resolution without exposing private state
- **Message integrity** — all messages signed by the sending Agent

VinculNet does not:
- Route messages through a central server by default
- Expose an Agent's memory, tools, or internal state to peers
- Allow communication with unpaired Agents
- Guarantee delivery — that is the Coalition Contract's responsibility

VinculNet is the network substrate.
Vincul is the institutional layer on top of it.

Stage 1 implementation provides:
- **MessageEnvelope** — signed, integrity-protected message wrapper with domain-separated hashing
- **HelloMessage** — mutual handshake for identity binding (TOFU trust model)
- **PeerRegistry** — in-memory map of authenticated peers (principal_id → pubkey + connection)
- **VinculPeer** — symmetric async peer that can both listen and connect over WebSocket

See `spec/transport/VINCULNET.md` for the full transport specification.

---

### ProtocolPeer

A composition of VinculPeer and VinculRuntime that enables Vincul protocol
operations over the network.

A ProtocolPeer:
- Runs a local VinculRuntime for action validation and enforcement
- Broadcasts success receipts to all connected peers
- Verifies incoming receipts against local state (hash cross-checking)
- Silently rejects receipts with mismatched scope or contract hashes

ProtocolPeer is the unit of participation in a networked coalition.
Each Principal's Agent operates through a ProtocolPeer instance.

See `spec/transport/VINCULNET.md §8` for the specification.

---

### MessageEnvelope

The signed, integrity-protected wrapper for all VinculNet messages after
handshake. Contains sender/recipient IDs, a JCS-serialized payload,
domain-separated payload hash, and an Ed25519 signature over the metadata.

See `spec/transport/VINCULNET.md §1`.

---

### HelloMessage

The mutual handshake message exchanged once per connection to establish
identity binding between two peers. Contains the sender's principal ID,
public key, and an Ed25519 signature. Uses TOFU (trust-on-first-use)
trust model.

See `spec/transport/VINCULNET.md §2`.

---

### PeerRegistry

An in-memory map of authenticated VinculNet peers, populated after
successful handshake. Maps `principal_id → (pubkey, connection)`.
Rejects public key changes mid-session (fail-closed).

See `spec/transport/VINCULNET.md §3`.

---

### Negotiation

The structured exchange between two or more Agents to reach
a mutually acceptable proposal within the bounds of their
respective Coalition Contracts and Capabilities.

A Negotiation follows three phases:

1. **Constraint Exchange** — each Agent declares its constraints
   (availability, budget limits, preferences) without revealing
   information beyond what its Capability scope permits

2. **Proposal Cycle** — Agents exchange proposals and counterproposals
   until a mutually acceptable option is identified or the Negotiation
   is declared failed

3. **Commitment** — Agents issue a signed Commitment artifact,
   which triggers Receipt generation on all sides

A Negotiation that fails to reach Commitment produces a
Failure Receipt — documenting what was attempted and why it failed.
Failure is always visible. It is never silent.

---

### Commitment

A signed artifact produced at the end of a successful Negotiation,
representing the agreed outcome before execution.

A Commitment:
- Is signed by all participating Agents
- References the Coalition Contracts under which it was produced
- Triggers execution of the agreed action
- Triggers Receipt generation upon execution

A Commitment is not a Receipt.
A Commitment is intent formalized.
A Receipt is result recorded.

---

### Sub-coalition

A Coalition spawned by a parent Coalition to handle a bounded
sub-task within the parent's Purpose and Capability scope.

A Sub-coalition:
- Inherits a subset of the parent Coalition Contract's Capabilities
- Cannot exceed the parent Coalition's budget or scope
- Produces its own Receipts, which are visible to the parent Coalition
- Dissolves independently of the parent

Sub-coalitions enable delegation without loss of accountability.
The parent Coalition's audit trail includes all Sub-coalition actions.

---

### Dissolution

The clean termination of a Coalition upon:
- Purpose fulfillment
- Expiry timestamp reached
- Unanimous consent to early termination
- Triggering of a dissolution rule defined in the Contract

Upon Dissolution:
- All active Capabilities are revoked
- All pending Negotiations are cancelled with Failure Receipts
- A final Dissolution Receipt is issued to all Signatories
- No further actions may be taken under the Contract

Dissolution is not failure. It is completion.
A Coalition that dissolves cleanly has succeeded at being ephemeral.

---

### Revocation

The act of a Signatory withdrawing their consent from a Coalition Contract
within the terms that Contract defines.

Revocation:
- Is always available to a Signatory
- Takes effect within the timeframe defined by the Contract
- May trigger Dissolution if the remaining Signatories fall below quorum
- Produces a Revocation Receipt visible to all Signatories
- Does not retroactively invalidate Receipts already issued

Revocation is not exit. It is boundary enforcement.
The right to revoke is what makes consent meaningful.

---

### Audit Trail

The complete, ordered, immutable set of Receipts produced
by a Coalition over its lifetime.

An Audit Trail:
- Is symmetric — identical for all Signatories
- Is append-only — Receipts are never removed or altered
- Is portable — exportable in a standard format
- Survives Dissolution — accessible after the Coalition terminates

The Audit Trail is the Coalition's memory.
It is the answer to the question: "What happened?"
It is also the answer to: "Who authorized it?"

---

### Compliance Harness

A test suite that verifies whether an implementation correctly
conforms to the Vincul specification.

Any Agent, Coalition runtime, or VinculNet implementation that passes
the Compliance Harness may describe itself as Vincul-conformant.

The Compliance Harness is the enforcement mechanism of the open protocol.
It replaces the need for a central authority to certify implementations.

## Terms Deliberately Excluded

The following terms are intentionally absent from the Vincul vocabulary.
Their absence is a design decision.

| Excluded Term | Why |
|---|---|
| **User** | Replaced by Principal — implies agency and accountability |
| **Admin** | No administrative authority exists in Vincul |
| **Permission** | Replaced by Capability — more precise, hierarchical |
| **Smart Contract** | Avoids blockchain connotations; Coalition Contracts are not on-chain |
| **Automation** | Agents act within boundaries; automation implies unbounded execution |
| **Seamless** | Seamlessness hides consent; Vincul makes consent visible |
| **Autonomous** | Agents are bounded, not autonomous |

---

*Vocabulary is architecture.
Name things precisely and the implementation follows.
Name things loosely and ambiguity becomes a feature.*
